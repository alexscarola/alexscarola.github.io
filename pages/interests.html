Why I Chose Education as my Minor?

I love my Computer Science major, but when it came to chosing my minor, I wanted to pick something that could leverage my major further than its general course. I chose Education because I think there is an importance with being able to share you knowledge with others. Throughout life you will find yourself constantly mentoring and helping others inside your field, and my education minor allows me to support the people around me best as I am able to.

My education minor was just a way to give back to the community, and there are a lot of useful tools that come along with it. I am a better communicator due to this minor, a more patient and understanding individual. It has allowed me to grow some of my other skills that are not a big focus in the computer science field. You may understand how something works, but when it comes to sharing that with an individual who has little knowledge on that matter, it is tougher than you think.

Fieldwork in Boulder

Throughout my education minor journey I have been granted multiple opportunities to do fieldwork with local programs. This partnership allowed me to support the staff members and youth in the program as well as gather research for a fieldwork analysis on the psychology of youth in educational settings.

The first program I worked with was The Boulder Housing Project, which was an after school program. Since it was a small site, I typically worked with two to three highschoolers where we focused on where they were in life.

The second program I worked with was Boulder Preparatory School. This was during school hours, and they have a nontraditional way for their typical school days. I would be assigned an activity to help out with and it could be anything from face painting to horror story workshops to basic car knowledge talks. I hope my work at both of these settings was as benefical for them as it was for me.

Education Internship

Save Our Youth is a non profit organization that I interned at over the summer of 2021. This program acted as a summer school for disadvantaged kids who needed extra help in certain areas of school, or just needed a support system outside of their home.

This program had a vast range of ages at many different levels of learning. Alongside supporting them in their school endevours we also made it a point to share with them all the different career paths they could take in the future, and had discussions about each one of them. We also got the opportunity to share skills outside of their general education. I got the chance to talk about computer science, and lead a small activity relating to artifical intellegence.

Throughout the summer I was able to watch these kids grow emotionally and intellectually as well as create some great relationships with the kids. Sometimes all you need is a friend and some empathy to get you through your day. There were many chaotic stories to come out of it, but it was always an opportunity for me to grow, and them to learn.
